The Thermocol Crisis

Non-Biodegradable: EPS (Thermocol) takes ~2000 years to degrade
Recyclability: Less than 2% is effectively recycled
Marine Pollution: Harms aquatic ecosystems and food chains
UN Warning: Plastic could outweigh fish by 2050 if unchecked

Our Sustainable Solution

We introduce Mycelium-Based Packaging, derived from mushroom roots and agricultural waste like paddy field leftovers. Here's how it's made:

Agricultural waste is treated with mushroom mycelium cultures
Mycelium spreads, forming a dense, dough-like structure
The mixture is molded into desired shapes
Stored for 4 days to solidify
Baked to kill spores, creating durable packaging
Final product is strong, light, and 100% biodegradable

Key Features of Mycelium Packaging

Biodegrades in 30–60 days (vs. Thermocol’s 2000 years)
Lightweight yet strong enough for shipping
Shock-absorbent — ideal for electronics & glass
Compostable — safe for the soil
Reduces packaging box size by 15–20%
